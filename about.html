SINCE 1943?

In 1899, Hiroshi Ashida, a kind and humble man from a small Japanese village, believed recipes were treasures meant to be shared. By 1943, he hosted weekly gatherings, sharing and exchanging his carefully crafted dishes with neighbors, fostering unity during hard times. In 2024, Emiko Ashida, Hiroshi’s great-granddaughter, launched Menu, a Japanese cuisine website inspired by his philosophy. Using his rediscovered recipe books, Emiko continues his legacy of sharing, connecting people through food across generations. The website’s tagline, “Recipes Connect Generations,” honors Hiroshi’s enduring vision.
