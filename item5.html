Katsudon

Recipe from Matthew C. Paraiso

Ingredients

Ingredient Measurement
Boneless Pork Chop 1 pc | 1/2 inch thick
Salt and Paper 1-2 pinches
All-Purpose Flour 1/4 cup
Egg (beaten) 1
Pank Breadcrumbs 1/2 Cup
Cooking Oil Frying
Onion (sliced) 1/2
Egg (beaten, for sauce) 1
Soy Sauce 1/4 cup
Mirin 1 tbsp
Dashi or Water 1/2 cup
Cooked Rice Bowl

Instructions

1. Prepare and Cook the Pork Cutlet
  1. Season the pork: Lightly season both sides of the pork chop with salt and pepper.

  2. Coat the pork: Dredge the pork chop in flour, dip it into the beaten egg, and coat it thoroughly in panko breadcrumbs.

  3. Fry the cutlet: Heat about 1/2 inch of oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the breaded pork cutlet for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and fully cooked (internal temperature of 145°F/63°C).

  4. Rest and slice: Remove the cutlet from the oil and let it rest on paper towels for a minute. Slice it into strips.

2. Katsudon Sauce
  1. Prepare the sauce: In a separate pan, combine soy sauce, mirin, and dashi. Bring it to a simmer.

  2. Cook the onions: Add the sliced onions to the sauce and cook until soft, about 3 minutes.

  3. Add the egg: Pour the beaten egg over the onions and sauce, and let it cook gently until the egg is just set (about 2-3 minutes).

3. Assemble the Katsudon
  1. Prepare the rice: Place a serving of warm rice into a bowl.

  2. Layer the pork cutlet: Lay the sliced pork cutlet over the rice.

  3. Add the egg mixture: Spoon the cooked onion and egg mixture over the pork cutlet.

  4. Serve: Garnish with optional green onions or seaweed, and enjoy!
